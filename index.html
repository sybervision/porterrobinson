Porter Robinson

Look at the Sky

Porter Robinson rocketed to stardom as a teenage EDM producer before his music evolved into an ambitious, introspective form of futuristic electropop. He first reached mainstream chart success with vibrant electro-house tracks like 2012's "Language," a U.K. Top Ten hit.

Look at the Sky

Growing up in the sleepy town of Chapel Hill, North Carolina, Porter began producing music at the tender age of 13, having caught the bug from Dance Dance Revolution. Fast forward several years later, and releases such as “Say My Name” were quickly attracting attention from all the right places.

"Look at the Sky" builds off these last three videos, with Robinson now in the "studio" of his mind. He's surrounded by these cloaked figures, aiding him in the creation of the single. These figures were actually shown in "Mirror" as guides and now have taken physical form. This type of artistry is genius and proves we are witnessing Robinson at the top of his game.

He continues, "the "Look at the Sky" video is about the beauty I find in this cycle. where the meaning of the song is more directly about the value of hope and resilience in the face of despair, with the video, i wanted to extend the idea of 'being alive next year' from being literal into being a metaphor about the things we can contribute to this world that will outlast us."
